Forward Geolocation

Forward geolocation, also simply known as geocoding, is the process of converting a descriptive location (like an address) into a specific geographical location on the Earth's surface. This is typically done by translating the address into a pair of coordinates, most commonly latitude and longitude.

Reverse Geolocation

Reverse geolocation, also known as address lookup, is the opposite of forward geolocation. It's the process of converting geographic coordinates (latitude and longitude) into a human-readable description of a location.
